Biography

Sarah Conniff is a coloratura soprano, specializing in operatic repertoire. Her performance credits include the role of Cunegonde in Leonard Bernstein's Candide, Calisto in La Calisto by Francesco Cavalli both with the DePaul Opera Theater, as well as La Fée in Pauline Viardot’s chamber opera Cendrillon with the Lyric Opera of Orange County, and The Queen of the Night in Mozart’s Die Zauberflöte with the Vienna Summer Music Festival, as well as partial or semi-staged adaptations of Sophie in Der Rosenkavalier, Belinda in Dido and Aeneas, Susanna in The Marriage of Figaro, Zerlina in Don Giovanni, Adina in L’elisir d’Amore, and Madame Herz in Der Schauspieldirektor. On the concert stage, she has appeared as soprano soloist in Mozart's Requiem K. 626 with the DePaul Symphony Orchestra, soprano soloist in selections from Pergolesi’s Stabat Mater with the Bob Cole Conservatory Collegium Musicum ensemble, and as a featured soloist with the DePaul University Early Music Ensemble in Vivaldi’s Gloria. She has competed in operatic singing competitions across the country– she was named a winner of the Washington District in the Metropolitan Opera National Council Auditions, awarded first place in Opera Modesto’s Kristina Townsend Memorial Competition, and won second place in the Chicago Chapter of the National Association of Teachers of Singing Student Auditions. Ms. Conniff is just as much at home on the choral stage as she is as a soloist; she toured Europe, competed in international competitions, premiered works by famed choral composer Jake Runestad, and recorded an album with the Bob Cole Conservatory of Music Chamber Choir, where she was frequently featured as a soloist. She has numerous performances of major choral works under her belt, including the Mozart and Duruflé Requiem, Haydn’s Nelson Mass, Mozart’s Great Mass in C Minor, Mendelssohn’s Elijah, and Morten Lauridsen’s Lux Aeterna among others.

She began her musical studies at age 10, playing violin, piano, and guitar before formally beginning her studies in classical voice. She graduated from DePaul University in Chicago with a Master of Music degree in Vocal Performance and received her Bachelor of Music degree from Bob Cole Conservatory of Music at California State University, Long Beach. She has also attended several study programs in the United States, as well as abroad including the American Institute of Musical Studies in Graz and the Vienna Summer Music Festival. She began her choral career in Fresno as a member of Heather Bishop’s choirs at Clovis North High School, and even first began her musical studies as a violinist in the Granite Ridge Middle School and Clovis North High School orchestras.

Repertoire

Sarah is experienced in performing a variety of genres including Clasical, Choral, Liturgical/Sacred, Contemporary Commercial, and Jazz, among others.
